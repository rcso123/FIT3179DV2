Effect of corruption and the accessibility of clean water

by Roland Chua Siew Ong

In this study, we would be discussing to find out if there is any correlation between corruption of a country with the accessibility of the citizens obtaining safe drinking water. This would be useful to see if corruption could be the cause of why the country could not be able to access safe drinking water.

The Corruption Perception Score and Safe Access to Drinking Water for top 5 and bottom 5 countries

This graph would be in charge of showing the Corruption Perception Score in each of the countries chosen. We would be taking the Top 5 countries with the highest score and the 5 countries with lower scores. The score denotes how corrupted is a country, being 100 very clean and 0 which is highly corrupted. On the other hand, the other bar would be showing the clean water access. As we can see from the graph, the ones with the bigger CPI values have a higher population with access to clean water. On the other hand, countries with the lower CPI scores have a smaller population which have access to clean water.

Relationship between the Corruption Perception Index and Access to clean water index

With all of the countries placed in a scatterplot, it is clear that the majority of the countries with a lower CPI score has a lower population in which has access to clean and safe drinking water. With the trend line inserted in the graph, it could be seen that the graph suggests that the HIGHER the CPI score, the HIGHER the population would have access to clean water.
